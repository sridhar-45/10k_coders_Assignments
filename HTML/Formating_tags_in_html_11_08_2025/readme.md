🔹 What are formatting tags?

**Formatting tags are specific HTML tags that change the appearance or style of text or content, without affecting the overall structure of the page.
They are mostly inline elements and focus on how content looks (bold, italic, underline, superscript, etc.).**

👉 Examples of formatting tags:

<b> – bold text

<i> – italic text

<u> – underlined text

<sub> – subscript (H₂O)

<sup> – superscript (x²)

<mark> – highlighted text

<small> – smaller text

<del> – deleted text (strikethrough)

<ins> – inserted text (underline)

<strong> – important text (semantically bold)

<em> – emphasized text (semantically italic)



**🔹 What are structural tags?**

**Other tags in HTML (like <div>, <header>, <section>, <table>, <form>) define the structure and layout of the document, not just formatting.**
For example:

<table> → creates a table structure

<form> → creates a form for input

<ul>, <ol>, <li> → lists

<h1> to <h6> → headings (these are not formatting tags; they are semantic/structural, though they affect appearance by default)
